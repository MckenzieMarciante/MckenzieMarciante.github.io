Project 1: Autoethnography

One of the most interesting assignments in this first unit, leading up to this project, was a podcast on Yik Yak and racism. Both the recording and the article over “How Social Media is Reshaping News” were absolutely fascinating. The two pieces stirred my interest and compelled me to write my paper about Yik Yak and racism on TCU’s campus, as well as about my own use of Twitter as a source for news.

 

In order to make craft this piece as autoethnographical, I had to focus primarily on incorporating my own personal experience—a surprisingly difficult and unfamiliar, yet beneficial, task for me to tackle.

 

Overall, this project helped me become more aware of my own social media usage and, especially, the effect of social media on my perceptions about society and current events. After completion of the project, I wondered whether social media effectively broadens my cultural knowledge (through exposure to honest, first-hand accounts), or corrupts the accuracy of reports I view, with unavoidable subjectivity.
