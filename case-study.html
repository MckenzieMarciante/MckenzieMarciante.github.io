Personal Statement

Project 2: Case Study

Through the execution of this assignment, I learned how easily someone can become villainized through social media, as well as how fast different situations on social media (especially Twitter) change and, even, fade out. I found fascinating the rapidity of the descent of Hal Daub from esteemed Regent of University of Nebraska to defamed non-supporter of athletes against racism.

 

The Twitter conversation developed fast enough to reach the trending charts—a feature I came to understand through my research for this project—yet fizzled out, even before the completion of my research.

 

My research on the Hal Daub hashtag and dialogue on Twitter also revealed a lot about the whole concept of social media as a forum for response. I think I take for granted how often events, even those which seemingly started on social media, really have long backgrounds with chains of events that led up to the initiation of a rhetorical situation on social media.

 

Ultimately, this project expanded my understanding of social media trends and affordances. Twitter, for example, seems to provide opportunity for interaction with controversial topics yet, also, seems to move too quickly for critical social discourse to be completed.
