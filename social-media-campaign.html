Personal Statement

My social media campaign served to raise support for the TCU’s men’s basketball team. I wanted to craft an argument for the importance of fan support, not in the form of monetary donation but, rather, of increased attendance.

 

I played greatly with emotional appeal, and I used the pathos of personal testimonies/interviews to convince audiences to believe the number of fans can (and does) directly affect the players. I also incorporated some information about the positive effects of fan support on fans, themselves.

 

Aside from learning how to create and edit media—such as a Facebook page, Twitter account, and promotional video—with a specific (and non-personal) purpose, I gained much from this project. I really enjoyed the process of creating the media, especially the video, but I also learned about the importance of targeting a specific audience and remaining aware of different appeals (and their effectiveness) in marketing and persuasion. I also got the opportunity to expand my experience in journalistic research, as I utilized a media pass to attend a press conference after one of the games, in order to obtain footage for my video.

 

In general, this project successfully peeked my interest in social media campaign work and raised my awareness of the relationship between social media and marketing. Now, after completion of this project, I am more interested in working in the fields of marketing and journalism.
